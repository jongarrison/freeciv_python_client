# Freeciv Turn Guide

## Turn Thought Process (Every Turn)

1. **Assess threats** — check borders, enemy unit movements, and city defenses
2. **Review cities** — confirm production queues, address unhappiness or starvation
3. **Move units** — scouts first, then workers, then military, then settlers
4. **Research** — verify the current tech target aligns with your strategy
5. **Diplomacy** — respond to AI proposals; consider trades, alliances, or war
6. **Gold** — adjust tax/science/luxury sliders if running a deficit or surplus

---

## Player Archetypes

### Expansionist
**Goal:** Found as many cities as possible early.
- Send settlers to high-yield tiles (rivers, grassland, resources) immediately
- Build a second city as fast as possible, then chain outward
- Prioritize: Alphabet → Ceremonial Burial → early Granaries
- Keep a warrior or two escorts to protect settlers from barbarians

### Militarist
**Goal:** Dominate opponents through conquest.
- Rush Bronze Working → Warrior Code → Iron Working
- Build barracks first in production cities
- Attack weakly defended cities; raze or capture based on location value
- Watch happiness — war weariness drains morale quickly

### Scientist
**Goal:** Win via technological supremacy (space race or cultural dominance).
- Max out science slider early, keep gold neutral
- Prioritize: Writing → University → Libraries in every city
- Research beeline: Philosophy (for free tech), then head toward Scientific Method
- Trade techs diplomatically to stay ahead without falling behind on gold

### Diplomat / Trader
**Goal:** Win through diplomacy, sabotage, and economic leverage.
- Establish embassies early; knowledge is power
- Prioritize: Alphabet → Trade → Banking
- Use trade routes to flood your treasury, then buy units rather than build
- Bribe/subvert enemy cities with diplomats and spies late-game

### Tall Builder
**Goal:** Win with a small number of highly developed cities.
- Settle only premium sites; quality over quantity
- Stack: Granary → Aqueduct → Library → Market → Temple in each city
- Avoid spreading too thin; invest in Wonders that scale with city size
- Play defensively and make friends — you can't fight everyone

## General Rules of Thumb

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| City growing into unhappiness | Build Temple or adjust luxury slider |
| Gold going negative | Lower science slider or disband idle units |
| Isolated continent | Expand freely; delay diplomacy until contact |
| Neighbor building military | Respond in kind or sue for peace early |
| Tech lead | Don't share — trade only for things you can't research faster |
| Late game | Every turn counts — queue production tightly, no idle cities |
